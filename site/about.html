About

Working with Trauma training session, Nepal Children's Art Museum for Art Refuge UK, March 2015

Jess Linton is an HCPC registered Art Psychotherapist, Visual Artist and Creative Practitioner.

She has been working creatively in community; arts; social action; education; health settings with all ages for over 15 years. Across this work she has witnessed the healing power of the arts and in 2011, made the decision to train as an art psychotherapist (qualifying in 2014, University of Hertfordshire). She is an advocate for a place for play and curiosity, as a basic human right for helping us to explore and make sense of ourselves, our relationships and the world around us.

This is at the heart of her arts practice, which includes developing socially engaged projects and creative collaborations. In 2019 she founded a wellbeing space in Stanmer Park, Sussex, and is currently leading on a collective of mental health professionals to establish stabilisation, environmental arts, growing and ecotherapy projects. Since her early art practice (c. 2000), she has been interested in the sculptural qualities of the natural world as well as the themes it evokes around: relationships, cycles and the temporariness of organic matter in correlation with our own experiences. Her recent inquiry involves digging for and working with local clay.

Therapeutic Portfolio

  • Sussex Partnership NHS Foundation Trust: Specialist CAMHS (Unaccompanied Asylum Seeking Children's social care team); CAMHS assertive outreach team Tier 3
  • East London NHS Foundation Trust: Psychiatric Inpatient Child & Adolescent Mental Health (CAMHS) Tier 4 (12yrs–18yrs)
  • Community Child & Adolescent Wellbeing Service (CAMHS) Tier 2 (mild to moderate risk, 4yrs–24yrs) and Tier 3 (specialist, 14–24yrs)
  • Wellbeing Lead / Senior Practitioner: Women, children & young people who have experienced domestic abuse
  • Psychosocial support for refugee & asylum seeking community, Art Refuge UK (community; UK, Northern France, Nepal)
  • Therapeutic Case Unit, leading Refugee Service (community; young adult, adult)
  • Local creative collaborations, arts + health groups and psycho-social spaces
  • Private practice (studio-based and out of doors)

Areas of Expertise

  • Adolescents with a range of mental health and developmental disorders: ADHD; ASD; borderline personality disorder, psychosis, schizophrenia, bi-polar, anxiety, depression
  • Young people with emotional difficulties, challenges around relationships; family; identity; personal development (NEET)
  • Refugee & asylum seeking community (UK and overseas in context of political conflict and social upheaval)
  • Complex trauma; post-traumatic stress and multi-layered mental health difficulties related to: early childhood; domestic abuse; displacement, persecution, loss and separation
  • Freelance Practice Educator (IATE; University of Hertfordshire; British Association of Art Therapy; London Art Therapy Centre)
  • Certified Clinical Supervisor

Through the breadth of her work, Jess is committed to supporting equality & diversity and continues to work with and learn from individuals from a range of ethnic, cultural, social, religious and political backgrounds.
