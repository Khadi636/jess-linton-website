# Design Options — Jess Linton Website

**How to use the preview page:**
Open `client-preview.html` in your browser. Click any option on the left panel and the page updates instantly. Try as many combinations as you like — nothing is saved until you click "Copy my choices."

**Keyboard shortcuts:** `P` = cycle palettes · `T` = cycle typography · `H` = cycle hero · `G` = cycle gallery · `B` = cycle buttons

---

## Colour Palettes

### Option A — Warm Editorial *(current site)*
**What it feels like:** Warm, grounded, handmade-paper quality. The kind of colour you'd find in a well-made art book or independent gallery.

- Background: soft off-white with a warm cream tone
- Brand colour: deep forest green
- Accent: terracotta / sand
- Text: near-black

**Best for:** Keeping the warmth and craft feel of the existing site. Familiar to anyone who has already seen your work. Works well for both the therapy and art sides of the practice.

---

### Option B — Cool Slate
**What it feels like:** Calm, professional, slightly more clinical. The kind of palette used by contemporary wellness practitioners or mental health organisations.

- Background: cool light grey
- Brand colour: deep navy
- Accent: soft lavender / muted purple
- Text: near-black with a blue-grey tone

**Best for:** If you want the therapy side to feel more formally professional. The cooler tones read as trustworthy and measured. Less "warm artist studio," more "trusted practitioner."

---

### Option C — Botanical Dark
**What it feels like:** Dramatic, distinctive, gallery-quality. A dark-mode palette with natural greens. Would make you stand out significantly from other art therapists' websites.

- Background: very dark near-black with a green undertone
- Brand colour: medium leaf green
- Accent: sharp lime-green
- Text: soft cream/white

**Best for:** If you want the site to lead with your artist identity rather than the therapy practice. Gallery artists, curators and arts organisations often respond very well to dark-mode sites. More unusual choice — may feel too bold for some therapy referrals.

---

## Typography

### Option A — Fraunces + Inter *(current site)*
**What it feels like:** The heading font (Fraunces) has an organic, slightly irregular quality — it was designed to evoke handwriting and natural forms. The body text (Inter) is clean, modern and very readable at small sizes.

- Headlines: Fraunces — warm, optical serif with personality
- Body: Inter — neutral, highly readable sans-serif

**Best for:** The combination already works well. Fraunces is distinctive without being precious, and Inter keeps the body text professional and clean.

---

### Option B — Playfair Display + Lora
**What it feels like:** More classical and literary. Both fonts are serifs — the headings are elegant and high-contrast, the body is warm and readable. The combination has a more traditional editorial feel, closer to a literary magazine or book publisher.

- Headlines: Playfair Display — high-contrast classical serif, elegant italics
- Body: Lora — warm, flowing serif with good rhythm at reading size

**Best for:** If you want the site to feel more literary and bookish. Works especially well if you write a lot of long-form content. May feel slightly more formal than Option A.

---

## Hero Layouts

The hero is the very first thing visitors see — the full-screen opening of the homepage.

### Option A — Split Screen *(current site)*
**What it looks like:** The screen is divided exactly in half — your artwork fills the left side as a photograph, and the heading text sits on a dark green background on the right.

**What it feels like:** Confident and structured. The image and text are given equal weight. The dark green panel grounds the site in your brand colour immediately.

**Best for:** Making a strong visual first impression with both your work and your identity at the same moment.

---

### Option B — Centred with Full-Screen Image
**What it looks like:** A large photograph fills the entire screen edge-to-edge, with a gentle dark overlay. Your heading text sits centered on top of the image, large and bold.

**What it feels like:** Immersive and cinematic. The image is given maximum impact. Your words appear to float over your work.

**Best for:** If you have a particularly striking photograph that you want to lead with. Works very well for art and community practice photography. The full-bleed image creates a strong emotional impression immediately.

---

### Option C — Type Only (No Hero Image)
**What it looks like:** Clean white/paper background, very large heading text, a decorative ruled line, and your description. No photograph at all in the opening section.

**What it feels like:** Minimal, editorial, confident. Puts your words and positioning statement at the centre of attention. Looks more like a design studio or independent practitioner than a traditional website.

**Best for:** If you want the writing to come first. Also loads fastest and works best on slow mobile connections. Some visitors find this more approachable — no large photograph means nothing can misread the tone before they've read anything.

## Gallery Styles

### Option A — Masonry *(current site)*
**What it looks like:** Images are arranged in columns but at their natural heights — taller images stay tall, wider images stay wide. The columns lock together like a mosaic.

**What it feels like:** Organic and natural. Each artwork is shown at its intended proportions. Creates a sense of abundance and variety.

**Best for:** A mixed collection of different formats and proportions, which describes your work well.

---

### Option B — Square Grid
**What it looks like:** Every image is cropped into a perfect square. The grid is even and structured, like an Instagram profile.

**What it feels like:** Ordered, precise, contemporary. The uniformity creates a calm rhythm. The trade-off is that some images are cropped — the viewer doesn't see the full composition.

**Best for:** If consistency and visual calm are more important than showing each piece in full. Works well if most of your images are roughly square already.

---

### Option C — Film Strip
**What it looks like:** Images are arranged in a single horizontal row at a tall, portrait format. Visitors scroll sideways to move through them.

**What it feels like:** Like flicking through slides or turning pages. Gives each work a moment of focus as it comes into view. Creates a gallery-walk feeling.

**Best for:** A curated selection of key pieces rather than a large collection. Draws visitors' attention to each image individually rather than scanning the whole grid at once.

---

## Button Styles

### Option A — Minimal Outline *(current site)*
**What it looks like:** A clean rectangular outline with no fill. When you hover, the rectangle fills with dark ink.

**What it feels like:** Restrained and graphic. A design-conscious choice that doesn't shout. Common in editorial design and contemporary art spaces.

---

### Option B — Rounded Pill
**What it looks like:** The same outline button but with fully rounded ends — a capsule or pill shape.

**What it feels like:** Friendlier and more approachable. Slightly more contemporary. Often feels more inviting for a healthcare or wellbeing context.

---

### Option C — Underline Only
**What it looks like:** No border or background at all — just the text with a coloured line underneath, like a link that looks intentional.

**What it feels like:** Very minimal and editorial. The most understated option. Makes the page feel less "website" and more like a designed document or publication.

**Best for:** If you want the text and images to breathe and the navigation to feel secondary. Works best paired with the Type Only hero (Option C).

## How to send your choices

Once you have decided on your preferences:

1. Open `client-preview.html` in your browser
2. Select each option you prefer
3. Click **"Copy my choices"** in the bottom-left panel
4. Paste the copied text into an email and send it

Your message will look like:

```
My design choices for jesslinton.co.uk:

Colour palette: Warm Editorial
Typography:     Fraunces + Inter
Hero layout:    Centred + Image
Gallery style:  Square Grid
Button style:   Rounded Pill
```

There are no wrong answers — these are all directions that could work well for your practice. If you're unsure between two options, note both and we can discuss before anything is changed.

Nothing on the live website will change until you confirm your choices.
