Privacy Policy

1. Who we are

The data controller for this website is Jess Linton, HCPC Registered Art Psychotherapist, Visual Artist and Creative Practitioner, based in Stanmer Park, Sussex, UK.

Contact: jess.a.linton@gmail.com
Location: Stanmer Park, Sussex, UK

2. What data we collect and why

We collect and use personal data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Contact enquiries: When you email or phone Jess directly, we receive your name, email address and/or phone number, and any information you choose to include. Legal basis: legitimate interests (responding to your enquiry).
  • Therapy referrals: When a professional or individual makes a referral, we receive the referred person's name, a brief background, and contact details. Legal basis: legitimate interests. Explicit consent will be obtained from the individual before any therapeutic work begins.
  • Website logs: This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may log your IP address and browser type for security and performance purposes. We do not access individual log entries. See GitHub's Privacy Statement for details.
  • Google Fonts: This website loads typefaces from fonts.googleapis.com. Your IP address is shared with Google when the page loads. See Google's Privacy Policy for details.

3. What we do not do

We do not:

  • Use Google Analytics or any other analytics or tracking service
  • Run advertising of any kind
  • Sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes
  • Build profiles of website visitors
  • Use automated decision-making or profiling

4. Cookies and local storage

This website does not set advertising or tracking cookies. We store your cookie consent preference in your browser's local storage under the key jl_cookie_consent. This data never leaves your device. See our Cookie Policy for full details.

5. How long we keep your data

  • Contact enquiry emails are kept for up to 2 years, or until they are no longer needed for the purpose for which they were provided.
  • Therapy records are retained in accordance with HCPC guidelines — typically 7 years after the end of therapeutic work, or 25 years for records relating to children and young people.

6. Your rights under UK GDPR

Under UK data protection law, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Rectification of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure ('right to be forgotten') where applicable
  • Restriction of processing in certain circumstances
  • Data portability where processing is based on consent or contract
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk

To exercise any of these rights, please contact Jess at jess.a.linton@gmail.com.

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or in applicable law. The date at the foot of this page shows when it was last updated. Continued use of this website after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

8. Contact us

If you have any questions about this policy or how we handle your data, please email jess.a.linton@gmail.com.
