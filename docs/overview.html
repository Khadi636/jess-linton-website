Overview

Creatively collaborating with a range of communities, groups and inspired individuals is a focus of all of Jess's work. Over the past decade and a half Jess has worked with: early years, families, children, young people, adults of all ages. She is experienced in working in a range of settings and contexts:

Settings & Contexts

Schools

Working with curriculum links, out of school hours learning, summer courses, Arts Award.

Special Educational Needs

Including artist training in working with children with mild to severe learning, mental and physical health difficulties.

Youth Groups

Working with young people from design to delivery of arts projects which support them to celebrate their sense of agency.

Adult Mental Health Centres

Long and short term artistic projects and skill sharing for adults in community mental health communities and centres, developed in collaboration with them and co-facilitating groups and workshops with service users.

Families

Open workshops; longer-term family learning projects; creative family events.

Hospitals

Summer courses; Hospital schools (Chelsea & Westminster), designing bespoke and one off workshops (Guys & St Thomas, Barts); Adolescent Psychiatric Units (Coborn, East London NHS Foundation Trust).

Refugee Networks

One off workshops, youth projects, community networks, supporting design, delivery and offering emotional support and signposting for unique projects such as: Parwaz VR; Refugee Tales; The Paper Project, public sessions, training.
