## Main Text

### Introduction
Jess Linton is an HCPC registered Art Psychotherapist, Visual Artist and Creative Practitioner.

She has been working creatively in community; arts; social action; education; health settings with all ages for over 15 years. Across this work she has witnessed the healing power of the arts and in 2011, made the decision to train as an art psychotherapist (qualifying in 2014, University of Hertfordshire). She is an advocate for a place for play and curiosity, as a basic human right for helping us to explore and make sense of ourselves, our relationships and the world around us.

This is at the heart of her arts practice, which includes developing socially engaged projects and creative collaborations. In 2019 she founded a wellbeing space in Stanmer Park, Sussex, and is currently leading on a collective of mental health professionals to establish stabilisation, environmental arts, growing and ecotherapy projects. Since her early art practice (c. 2000), she has been interested in the sculptural qualities of the natural world as well as the themes it evokes around: relationships, cycles and the temporariness of organic matter in correlation with our own experiences. Her recent inquiry involves digging for and working with local clay. In her work in Nepal during the devastating earthquakes (2015), natural materials became grounding tools during difficult conversations, and symbols of how small and temporary, yet astonishing and special life could be. Working with natural materials and the natural world offered rich discussion around important themes for Nepal at the time.

- Local creative collaborations, arts + health groups and psycho-social spaces

### Areas of Expertise

Jess has particular expertise and experience in working with:

- Adolescents with a range of mental health disorders and developmental disorders, such as: ADHD; ASD; borderline personality disorder, psychosis, schizophrenia, bi-polar, anxiety, depression (NHS in-patient and community settings / other)
- Young people with emotional difficulties, day-to-day challenges around relationships; family; identity; personal development (within mainstream education or not in education, employment or training (NEET)
- Refugee & asylum seeking community (UK and overseas in context of political conflict and social upheaval)
- Complex trauma; post-traumatic stress and other complex / multi-layered mental health difficulties related to: experiences in early childhood; domestic abuse; displacement, persecution, loss and separation
- Freelance Practice Educator (IATE; University of Hertfordshire; British Association of Art Therapy; London Art Therapy Centre; other)
- Certified Clinical Supervisor
